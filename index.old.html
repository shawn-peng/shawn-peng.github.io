Enumerating consistent sub-graphs of directed acyclic graphs: an insight into biomedical ontologies

Yisu Peng, Yuxiang Jiang, Predrag Radivojac

Yisu Peng has solved the problem of exact enumeration of all sub-graphs of a given directed acyclic graph (DAG). This is a non-trivial enumeration problem that has been solved for trees by Fred Ruskey in 1981 (Ruskey F. Listing and counting subtrees of a tree. SIAM J. Comput. 10, 141-151, 1981). Ruskey's work has been later extended to weighted trees but to our knowledge the solution for DAGs had not been proposed before our paper. Yisu proposed a recursive partitioning approach and together with another student on that project, Yuxiang Jiang, proved its correctness. He implemented the algorithm in C and evaluated it on multiple synthetic DAGs. Then he proposed several practical speed-ups that were necessary to apply this algorithm to large biomedical ontologies such as Gene Ontology that have over 30,000 nodes. The paper itself has another data analysis/bioinformatics component that was jointly handled by Yisu and Yuxiang Jiang. In terms of writing, Yisu contributed to the first draft of the paper. Besides the intrinsic appeal of solving a computer science problem, the instrumental importance of this paper was in characterizing the cardinality of the structured output space in protein function prediction and providing another perspective on why this prediction problem is so difficult. Yisu's work is going to gain traction and might be one of the contributing factors of reducing the sizes of the ontologies if the community is going to make further advances in this direction, which itself has a significant biomedical importance.
